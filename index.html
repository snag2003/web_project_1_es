The main problem with education

Whether in school or university, the one thing no one teaches you is how to teach yourself. We often end up doing specific exercises and memorizing information without thinking, just so we can pass the test. But once the test is over, that information is no longer useful and there’s no guarantee we’ll retain it. In the end, we're only learning how to pass exams.

Here’s the real question: does this repetition and memorization approach actually help us understand the subject? The current academic consensus says no. In reality, what's more important than any individual subject is knowing how to study. Yet, for some reason, this point is often overlooked. So what can we do to fix it?

Well, there are several techniques and exercises that can be applied to the learning process to help make it more effective and engaging. Keep going to discover some of the secrets of effective learning.

Learning Techniques

Five strategies by Barbara Oakley

Think in two different ways

Deep understanding can be achieved by alternating freely between focused and diffuse thinking.

Use recall

Improving your ability to recall key pieces of information helps connect different areas of the brain.

Interleave

When you learn multiple skills at the same time, they reinforce each other.

Ask questions

When you listen to your teacher, think of a good question—something that truly intrigues you.

Create the illusion of competence

Test yourself, record your result, share it with others, and repeat with different setups. Only then can you be sure you understand something.

TED Talks

For procrastination lovers

Barbara Oakley's story.

During her childhood, math didn’t come naturally to Barbara. She thought she was more of a humanities person and not particularly bright. Hoping to become a linguist, she enlisted in the army after high school, where she was assigned to learn Russian. After several years of service, during which she rose to the rank of captain, Barbara left the army and decided to study engineering. That’s when she developed her learning methodology, as she had to rewire her brain for a different kind of learning. It turns out that when something doesn’t come naturally to you, having to work hard to gain knowledge that others grasp quickly gives you a deeper, more complete understanding of the subject.
